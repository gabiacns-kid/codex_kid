# Government Ad Final PPT Style Reference

Use this reference when the user asks to make, revise, or critique Korean government-advertising proposal PPTs in the style of the 2026 H2 government-advertising final qualitative proposal deck.

This is a design and layout reference, not a content source. Preserve RFP logic and factual source checks separately.

## Deck DNA

- Format: very wide 16:9 canvas, approximately 22 x 12.37 inches.
- Tone: public-sector B2B proposal, polished but not decorative, evidence-led, operationally credible.
- Primary visual language: clean white slides with government-green emphasis, occasional dark section/impact slides, screenshots and real proposal artifacts as proof.
- Reading style: one large claim per slide, then proof blocks, diagrams, tables, or screenshots.
- Default posture: structured expertise rather than a marketing brochure.

## Typography

Use the same hierarchy when possible:

- Cover title: Paperlogy 6 SemiBold or equivalent Korean display type, around 66 pt.
- Main content slide title: Paperlogy 6 SemiBold or equivalent Korean display type, usually 32-36 pt.
- Section label at top-left: Paperlogy 6 SemiBold or equivalent Korean display type, around 20 pt.
- Top-left subsection label: Paperlogy 3 Light or equivalent light Korean display type, around 18 pt.
- Body: Pretendard / Pretendard Medium, usually 16-20 pt.
- Card or module header: Pretendard SemiBold, usually 20-24 pt.
- Small captions/footers/source notes: Pretendard Medium or Pretendard Light, around 9-14 pt.
- Use bold/semibold weight for role labels, step labels, and KPI terms.

Avoid mixing unrelated fonts unless copied from existing source logos/images. The visible system should mainly be Paperlogy-style display titles plus Pretendard body text.

## Color System

Use a restrained professional palette:

- Primary green: `#158B64`
- Secondary green: `#1BA673`
- Bright accent green: `#27D295`
- Deep blue/navy accent: `#014591`
- Blue-green support: `#116D4E`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Black/dark text: `#000000` or theme text black.
- Soft gray blocks: `#F1F4F8`, `#E6F0EC`, or very light theme gray.

Use green for government-search strategy claims, section emphasis, and key labels. Use blue/navy for solution/data/tool emphasis or platform-like elements. Do not let the slide become a multi-color dashboard.

## Repeated Page Frame

Most content slides should keep a predictable top-left navigation block:

```text
Top-left line 1: current major section, for example "05 Operation Strategy"
Top-left line 2: current subtopic, for example "(2) Media-specific KPI Operation"
Main title: large claim beneath the navigation
Subtitle/intro: one plain sentence explaining why the slide matters
Main body: proof object, diagram, table, screenshot, or comparison
Footer: optional tiny page number or project label; keep quiet
```

This top-left frame is part of the style. Do not replace it with centered generic title slides except for cover/contents/closing or deliberate section openers.

## Layout Archetypes

### 1. Claim + Proof Object

- Left/top: large title, usually green.
- Middle/right: screenshot, product screen, chart, or diagram.
- Bottom or side: 2-4 concise explanatory bullets.
- Use when explaining solutions, reports, tools, or system capabilities.

### 2. Step Process

- Use numbered steps or stage labels.
- Each step should have a short noun phrase, not a paragraph title.
- Support text should explain the operating action, not repeat the step name.
- Green circles/arrows and white blocks are acceptable, but avoid crowded card grids.

### 3. Media/KPI Matrix

- Use clear tables for media roles, KPI, product, and operation strategy.
- Header rows can use dark/green fills; body rows should remain highly readable.
- Keep each cell as a short phrase. Long explanations belong below the table or in notes.

### 4. As-Is / To-Be

- Strong for diagnosis and simulation slides.
- As-Is should identify the current limitation without sounding accusatory.
- To-Be should state the improved operating structure.
- Use screenshots or platform examples only as evidence, not as full-page clutter.

### 5. Reference Case

- Left: project name or anonymized category, short context.
- Right/bottom: analysis -> goal -> operation -> result.
- Use large, direct labels and avoid tiny dense paragraphs.

### 6. Impact/Dark Slide

- Used sparingly for cover, section transition, pain point, and closing.
- Dark or image-led background with white text and green accent.
- Make one claim dominate; do not fill the slide with many boxes.

## Content Density Rules

- One slide should explain one strategic point.
- Large titles can be 1-2 lines; avoid 3+ line titles unless this is a section opener.
- Keep body copy in short explanatory lines. If a sentence needs multiple clauses, convert it into a table row or diagram label.
- Screenshots should not be decorative. Pair each screenshot with a clear operational interpretation.
- When a slide contains a table, reduce surrounding prose.
- When a slide contains screenshots/images, use fewer bullets and stronger labels.

## Design Patterns To Preserve

- Top-left section/subsection labels are consistent and useful.
- Main titles often end in a noun phrase or short declarative phrase.
- Green title plus black body is the default.
- White background with a wide, spacious canvas is the default.
- Real platform screenshots, media screenshots, and result tables are treated as proof.
- Rounded rectangles exist, but they support process/table modules; they should not become generic decorative cards.
- Bottom summary captions can be used when they clarify the slide's operating meaning, but they should not compete with the title.

## Government Proposal Fit

This style works best for:

- RFP response decks.
- Public-sector keyword advertising proposals.
- Operating strategy, reporting, service support, and reference sections.
- KPI/Total KPI logic, media roles, risk management, and reporting structures.
- Simulation slides that need to look practical and evidence-based.

For government advertising decks, design should reinforce:

- reliability,
- official information delivery,
- KPI accountability,
- operational transparency,
- reporting and risk-management discipline.

## Avoid

- Replacing the top-left section system with a generic centered hero layout.
- Using too many colors or gradient decorations.
- Copying the WSE clean style too literally; this government style is wider, more sectioned, and more proof-heavy.
- Making every slide a 3-card grid.
- Overusing dark backgrounds. Dark slides should create punctuation, not become the whole deck.
- Filling screenshots without explaining the operational implication.
- Adding text in tiny 10-12 pt blocks except source/caption/footer.

## Practical Prompt Snippet

When asking an AI deck tool to match this style, use:

> 2026 government-advertising RFP proposal style: wide 16:9 Korean B2B proposal deck, white background, top-left section/subsection label on every content slide, large Paperlogy-style Korean title in green, Pretendard-style body text, restrained green/navy/gray palette, evidence-led layouts with screenshots, tables, process diagrams, and KPI matrices. Keep one message per slide, avoid decorative gradients and generic card grids, and use concise operational wording.
